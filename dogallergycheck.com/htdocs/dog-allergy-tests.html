Why is my dog constantly scratching and licking?
Most likely, your dog is suffering from food or environmental allergies. Common culprits include chicken, beef, dairy, wheat, and environmental factors like pollen or dust mites. These tests can identify exactly what's causing the discomfort so you can eliminate it from your dog's environment.
How accurate are these at-home tests compared to vet testing?
The tests we recommend have 85-95% accuracy compared to veterinary testing, but at a fraction of the cost. While vet testing costs $300-500, these tests range from $80-160 and provide valuable insights to guide your dog's diet and lifestyle changes.
How quickly will I see results after changing my dog's diet?
Most pet parents see improvement within 2-4 weeks of eliminating problematic foods. Some dogs show improvement in as little as 7-10 days. The key is consistency and completely removing the identified allergens from your dog's diet and environment.
What's the difference between an allergy and an intolerance?
Allergies involve the immune system and can cause severe reactions like hives, swelling, or even anaphylaxis. Intolerances cause digestive upset, skin irritation, or chronic inflammation without triggering the immune system. Both can cause your dog significant discomfort and should be addressed.
Do I need to stop my dog's current medications before testing?
No! Unlike some tests, the recommended kits don't require you to stop medications, supplements, or change your dog's diet before testing. This makes the process much easier and less stressful for both you and your pet.
